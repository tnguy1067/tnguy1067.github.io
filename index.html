Tiffany Nguyen

Pre-PA

Growing up in San Jose, one of the cities with the highest homelessness population, I have always wanted to find a way to serve those who don’t have access to essential resources, including health care. Although I am unable to directly deliver medical aid to these individuals, I have done what is tangible. Collaborating with organizations in my hometown, I have raised awareness and funds to help aid those who are unhoused. These funds have gone directly to making care packages that include the necessities for survival, such as clothing and food. Whether it's always having a care package set aside in my care to give to those in need or spreading awareness about what homelessness is. My interest to help serve the unhoused community comes from the constant misconception and negative connotation that stands behind the word “homeless.” These misconceptions have created a barrier for those unhoused without healthcare. With each unhoused individual is a story that is not being listened to. With this opportunity I want to strive to listen to these individuals, to not only give them a voice, but to help aid them in their health journey.

I have worked closely with the Registered Nurses Professional Association, RNPA, as an administrative assistant to help nurses advocate for their rights and professional development. This opportunity has given me profound insight on the various aspects of the nursing profession, while learning about ways to advocate for the next and upcoming generation of healthcare professions. Simultaneously, my journey as a medical assistant student at Silicon Valley Career Technical Education has given me hands on experience with injections, phlebotomy, vitals, laboratory tests, universal precautions, exam room procedures, and career readiness Both of these experiences have fostered an environment of leadership along with clinical experience, as I get to have a voice and be involved with community service that aligns with the healthcare world.

During my time at my current educational institution I have been apart of various clubs and organizations that have the goal to ultimately better the community and work towards a greater cause.
